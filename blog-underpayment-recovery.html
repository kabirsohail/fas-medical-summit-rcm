Underpayment is not the same as denial

A denied claim is visible. An underpaid claim can look closed unless someone compares allowed amounts, contract terms, payer policy, and expected reimbursement. That makes underpayment recovery one of the quieter revenue opportunities in RCM.

The challenge is prioritization. Not every variance is worth chasing, and not every contract interpretation is straightforward.

Start with expected reimbursement

Recovery work needs a baseline: what should have been paid, what was paid, why the payer reduced it, and whether the account has documentation to support an appeal or reconsideration.

For surgical specialties, the review may include bundled services, implants, multiple procedures, assistant surgeon rules, modifier policy, and payer-specific fee schedule behavior.

Prioritize by recoverability

A practical queue ranks accounts by balance, payer, variance size, documentation strength, timely filing risk, and known payer behavior. This keeps the recovery team focused on claims where action is likely to matter.

Leadership should also see repeated underpayment patterns, because a recurring payer behavior may justify contract discussion or a front-end billing edit.

Close the loop

A recovery program should not become a permanent clean-up crew. When a pattern is confirmed, update charge capture, coding review, payer rules, and reporting so the next claim is cleaner.
