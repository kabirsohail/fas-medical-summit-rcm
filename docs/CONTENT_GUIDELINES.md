# Content Guidelines

What FAS sounds like, what numbers we can use, and what we can't promise.

## Voice

**Confident but not boastful.** We don't say "we're the best." We say "we recovered $184,200 for one ASC last quarter."

**Specific over vague.** "32-day average A/R" beats "fast turnaround." "Up to 30% revenue growth for clients" beats "significant ROI."

**Operator-to-operator.** Our reader is a practice administrator, surgical center owner, or anesthesiologist who's been burned by a generic biller before. Talk to them like another operator who's seen the inside.

**No corporate jargon.** Don't write:
- "synergize," "leverage," "world-class," "best-in-class," "cutting-edge"
- "unlock value," "drive outcomes," "accelerate growth"
- "robust solution," "comprehensive suite," "end-to-end transformation"

Do write:
- "Patient Onboarding & Pre-Registration"
- "We work the aging buckets every Tuesday"
- "Most practices we onboard see meaningful collection lift within two quarters"

## Sentence case for headings

Most h2s and h3s use sentence case, not Title Case:
- ✓ "End-to-end revenue cycle."
- ✓ "Built for the disciplines that demand the most."
- ✗ "End-To-End Revenue Cycle."
- ✗ "The Numbers That Define Our Practice."

Title Case is reserved for proper nouns ("FAS Medical Summit," "Frisco, Texas") and short labels ("Free Consultation").

## Typography conventions

- **Em-dashes** (—) not double-hyphens (--). Surround with no spaces or thin spaces.
- **Curly quotes** ("smart quotes") in body copy. Straight quotes only in code samples.
- **Apostrophes** are curly: `don't`, `practice's`, never `don't`.
- **Numbers under 10** are spelled out in body copy, but verified business-history numbers may stay as digits for clarity ("25+ years", "formed in 2022").

## What we can't promise — Watch these words

These create legal exposure or HHS scrutiny:

| Word | Why it's risky | Use instead |
|---|---|---|
| "always" | Implies 100% performance | "consistently," "reliably" |
| "guaranteed" | Implies legal warranty | "we work to," "designed to" |
| "we promise" | Same problem | "we commit to," "you can expect" |
| "never lose" | Impossible to back up | "minimize losses to" |
| "100% [anything]" | Audit nightmare | Real percentage, or "high" |
| "best in industry" | Compared to what? | "FAS standard," "what we deliver" |
| "real-time dashboards" | Most aren't truly real-time | "transparent reporting," "continuously-updated" |
| "live operations" | Implies always-on monitoring | Just don't claim it |

**Exceptions** — these specific "real-time" usages ARE truthful and OK:
- "Real-time 270/271 eligibility verification" (literal EDI standard)
- "Real-time payer validation" (clearinghouse 277CA is real-time)
- Anything tied to a specific EDI transaction set

## Illustrative content (always labeled "EXAMPLE")

The 3D dashboard mockups in the Why-Us and Platform sections show sample data. These are **clearly tagged "EXAMPLE"** so a careful reader knows they're representative, not live:

- Sample dollar amounts: `$184,200`, `$142,860`, `$41,340`
- Sample chart trends (denial reduction, revenue growth)
- Sample integration partner names

This is acceptable because:
1. The "EXAMPLE" tag is visible in the mockup
2. The numbers are realistic for the industry
3. They're shown in dashboard chrome (browser dots, mono-font labels) that signals "this is a UI screenshot"

If the mockup loses its "EXAMPLE" tag, the content becomes a fabricated claim. Don't remove the tag.

## When in doubt

- **Vague-but-true beats specific-but-fabricated.** Better to say "many clients see meaningful improvement" than invent a 47.3% statistic.
- **Show the EXAMPLE tag.** Any number in a mockup-style UI must carry "EXAMPLE."
- **Ask the user before claiming a new stat.** If it's not on the canonical sheet, soften or omit until verified.
